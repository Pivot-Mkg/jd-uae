Cracking the Unfillable Role: How JDME Turned a 12-Month Hiring Stalemate into Strategic Success

When a leading GCC real estate developer struggled for over a year to fill a mission-critical role, VP of Strategy & FP&A, it wasn’t for lack of effort. Multiple search firms had cycled through the mandate, bound by a non-negotiable brief: candidates must possess both a Big 4 pedigree and proven GCC experience. Twelve months later, the vacancy remained. Strategic projects were slipping, leadership frustration was mounting, and momentum was at risk. The real blocker wasn’t the market, it was the brief.

Rigid criteria can cripple even the strongest searches. As “must-have” lists expand, the talent pool narrows and the search collapses into a waiting game. That’s precisely where James Douglas Middle East (JDME) began, not with rewriting the job description, but by reframing the challenge entirely.

Reframing the Mandate

JDME partnered directly with the senior leadership team to reset the assignment around outcomes, not optics. Together, they asked:

  • JDME partnered directly with the senior leadership team to reset the assignment around outcomes, not optics. Together, they asked:
  • Which capabilities would truly drive P&L performance and shape the board’s narrative?

From this dialogue emerged a sharper, execution-anchored brief: ownership of execution, deep FP&A and financial modelling expertise and credible investor relations skills to engage the market with authority. The shift moved the emphasis from resumes and logos to measurable business impact.

Re-Mapping the Market

With the new brief established, JDME built a tiered market map; broadening the pool beyond a single sector into adjacencies where the needed skills were forged and tested: strategy houses, buy-side roles and transformation offices.

This included proactive engagement with passive talent; senior operators not actively on the market but open to the right strategic opportunity.

JDME’s approach combined data with human intelligence:

  • Targeted outreach powered by the tiered map.
  • Influencer engagement to secure warm introductions.
  • Weekly iteration loops with the client, pressure-testing signals and adjusting course in real time.

This rhythm preserved speed without sacrificing fit.

What Changed and Why It Worked
  • Outcomes first, labels second: By centring execution, financial depth and IR credibility, JDME uncovered leaders a brand-locked search would have overlooked.
  • Talent gravity through narrative: Positioning the opportunity around strategic impact and fundraising drew in senior operators who were otherwise “not looking”.
  • Agile transparency: Weekly, data-rich check-ins gave the client confidence to relax non-critical filters and double down in areas where the market showed genuine traction.
The Result: Closur and Compounding Value

The search concluded with the appointment of a VP boasting over 15 years across strategy, valuations and investor relations. A leader equally adept at shaping long-term direction and rolling up sleeves for execution.

More than solving a 12-month stalemate, the appointment reset the trajectory of mission-critical initiatives by embedding accountable ownership at the centre of the mandate. For JDME, it demonstrated not only perseverance but also consultative strength and a problem-solving ethos; a replicable playbook for transforming “unfillable” roles into strategic wins.

Lessons Leaders Can Take Forward
  • Rewrite the scorecard: Anchor to outcomes, then work backward to essential capabilities. Pedigree is a proxy, not a gate.
  • Map adjacencies, not just titles: The best FP&A or IR leaders may already sit within strategy, transformation or buy-side teams outside your immediate sector.
  • Court the quiet stars: Your strongest hire may be a passive candidate. Design outreach for those too busy delivering impact to be updating their CVs.
  • Iterate in the open: Weekly, data-driven progress reviews build velocity and smarter decision-making.
